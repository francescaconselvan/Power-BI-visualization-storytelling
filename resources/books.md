# Books
This is a curated list of recommended books for mastering data visualization, storytelling, and Power BI.

---
## General overview
1. Cole Nussbaumer Knaflic, *Storytelling with Data*  
   Essential guide to creating compelling data stories and effective visualizations for business audiences. Covers storytelling principles, chart selection, and design best practices.  

2. Edward Tufte, *Envisioning Information*  
   Classic work on the visual display of information across various media. Explores principles of information design and how to present complex data clearly.  

3. Edward Tufte, *The Visual Display of Quantitative Information*
   Foundational text on data visualization theory and practice. Introduces key concepts like data-ink ratio and chartjunk, with numerous historical and contemporary examples.

4. William S. Cleveland, *The Elements of Graphing Data*
   Comprehensive guide to graphical data display principles based on research in human perception and cognition

---
## Technical 
1.  Sergei Kolokolov, *Data Visualization with Microsoft Power BI*
    Practical guide to creating effective visualizations    and dashboards in Power BI, covering technical     implementation and best practices.    

2. Steve Wexler, Jeffrey Shaffer, Andy Cotgreave, *The Big Book of Dashboards: Visualizing Your Data Using Real-World Business Scenarios*  
    Real-world dashboard examples across different industries and scenarios. Provides practical patterns and design solutions for common business needs with dozens of examples.

---
## Graphic design
1. Sandra Rendgen, Julius Wiedemann, *Information Graphics*
   Comprehensive visual reference showcasing the best infographics and data visualizations from around the world, exploring the history and evolution of information design.

2. Daniel Rosenberg, Anthony Grafton, *Cartographies of Time: A History of the Timeline*
   Explores the visual history of timelines and temporal representation, examining how time has been depicted graphically from ancient to modern eras.

3. Giorgia Lupi, Phillip Cox, *Speak Data: Artists, Scientists, Thinkers, and Dreamers on How We Live Our Lives in Numbers*
   Collection of perspectives from creative thinkers on data visualization and how we interpret and communicate our lives through data and visual narratives.
